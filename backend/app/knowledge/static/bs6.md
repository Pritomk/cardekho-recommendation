# BS6 Phase 2 (RDE Norms)

## Summary
BS6 Phase 2, also known as Real Driving Emissions (RDE) norms, ensures that vehicles meet strict emission limits in real-world driving conditions, not just in the laboratory.

## Detailed Explanation
Implemented in April 2023, BS6 Phase 2 requires cars to have an onboard self-diagnostic device that constantly monitors the emission levels of parts like the catalytic converter and oxygen sensors. If emissions exceed the threshold, a warning light illuminates. This essentially killed many small diesel engines in the market because upgrading them to meet RDE norms was too expensive.

## Common Misconceptions
- **"BS6 Phase 2 cars are slower."** Not necessarily. While some tuning changes were made, manufacturers often compensated to maintain driveability.
- **"It only affects diesel cars."** It affects petrol cars too, requiring them to be more precise with fuel injection and emission management.

## Buyer Advice
For city drivers, BS6 Phase 2 compliance means a cleaner environment but practically identical driving feel to Phase 1. However, if you are looking at diesel cars, Phase 2 compliant diesels are heavily engineered with DPF (Diesel Particulate Filters) and SCR systems. If your daily running is short city trips, a BS6 Phase 2 diesel might give you DPF clogging issues. Stick to petrol or hybrid for low/city running.
