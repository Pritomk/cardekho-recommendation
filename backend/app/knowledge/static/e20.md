# E20 Fuel Compatibility

## Summary
E20 fuel is a blend of 20% ethanol and 80% petrol. It is the new government standard being rolled out across India to reduce emissions and oil imports.

## Detailed Explanation
Starting from April 2023, all new vehicles manufactured in India must be material-compatible with E20 fuel. Ethanol is corrosive, so older vehicles not designed for E20 might experience accelerated wear on rubber hoses, fuel lines, and engine components if forced to use it continuously. Furthermore, ethanol has a lower energy density than pure petrol, which can lead to a minor drop in fuel efficiency (mileage).

## Common Misconceptions
- **"It will destroy my old car instantly."** No, but prolonged use over years will degrade rubber and plastic fuel components faster.
- **"It gives better mileage."** Actually, due to lower energy density, mileage might drop by 2-5% compared to pure petrol.

## Buyer Advice
If you plan to keep your new car for 8 to 10 years, E20 compliance is strictly mandatory. Buying a used car or an older BS6 model that is only E10 compatible is risky for long-term ownership, as E10 fuel might become harder to find or more expensive in the future.
